How to Prepare Print-Ready Files: A Step-by-Step Guide

November 2024 Waves Printing Press Team 8 min read

One of the most common causes of print delays and reprints in Kuwait is poorly prepared artwork files. A file that looks perfect on screen can produce blurry, colour-shifted, or poorly trimmed results when printed — costing you time, money, and frustration.

At Waves Printing Press, our pre-press team reviews every file we receive from Kuwait clients before production begins. This guide shares the exact checklist our team uses — so you can send print-ready files with confidence and avoid the most common mistakes.

The Print-Ready File Checklist

1

Set Up Correct Document Size with Bleed

Bleed is the extra area beyond the final trim size of your document. It prevents white edges appearing if the paper shifts slightly during cutting. Add 3mm of bleed on all four sides of your document. For an A5 flyer (148×210mm), your document with bleed should be 154×216mm. Keep all critical content — text, logos, faces — at least 3–5mm inside the trim line as a "safe zone."

2

Use CMYK Colour Mode (Not RGB)

RGB is a screen colour model. Print uses CMYK (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Key/Black). RGB files converted at print time can produce unexpected colour shifts — blues can become purple, and vibrant greens can look muddy. Always set your document to CMYK before designing. In Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, or InDesign: Document Setup → Colour Mode → CMYK.

3

Set Image Resolution to Minimum 300 DPI

Screen resolution is 72 DPI. Print requires 300 DPI (dots per inch) at the final print size. A low-resolution image will look crisp on screen but pixelated and blurry when printed. Always source or create images at 300 DPI. If you are unsure, right-click your image in Photoshop and check Image → Image Size.

4

Embed or Outline All Fonts

If your printer's computer doesn't have the same fonts installed, text will be substituted or disappear entirely. In Adobe Illustrator: Select All → Type → Create Outlines. In InDesign: export to PDF with "Embed Fonts" selected. In Photoshop: flatten all type layers before saving. PDF export with embedded fonts is the safest approach.

5

Export to Print-Ready PDF

PDF is the universally accepted print-ready format. When exporting from design software, use the "PDF/X-1a" or "PDF/X-4" standard. This ensures colours are CMYK, fonts are embedded, bleed is included, and the file is compatible with professional RIP software. In InDesign: File → Export → Adobe PDF (Print) → PDF/X-1a.

6

Check Black Text Settings

For small black text, use 100% K (black) only — not "rich black" (C:40 M:30 Y:30 K:100). Rich black on small text causes registration problems and blurry edges. Reserve rich black for large headlines, backgrounds, and shapes. Ask your Kuwait printing press team if unsure about the right setting for your specific job.

7

Include Crop Marks and Printer Marks

Crop marks show the printer exactly where to trim the final piece. When exporting your PDF, enable "Marks and Bleeds" → select "Crop Marks" and confirm your bleed values are set to 3mm. Most professional print buyers in Kuwait include these marks as standard — they make production faster and reduce errors.

  • High-resolution JPG/PNG — 300 DPI minimum, CMYK preferred

Common File Preparation Mistakes We See in Kuwait

  • RGB colour mode instead of CMYK (causes colour shift in print)
  • Low-resolution images (72 DPI screen resolution instead of 300 DPI print)
  • No bleed (causes white edge on trimmed pieces)
  • Text too close to the trim edge (risk of being cut off)
  • Fonts not embedded or outlined (text substitution or disappearance)
  • Incorrect black for text (rich black on small type causes blur)
  • Missing or incorrect dimensions (document setup at wrong size)

Frequently Asked Questions

What file format should I send to a printing press in Kuwait?
PDF is the preferred format for all print jobs at Waves Printing Press. Export your PDF with CMYK colours, embedded fonts, 3mm bleed included, and crop marks enabled. If you use Adobe Illustrator, InDesign, or Photoshop, we can also accept native files. Contact us if unsure.
Why does my printed colour look different from my screen?
Screens display colours in RGB (light-based), while printing uses CMYK (pigment-based). The gamut (range) of reproducible colours differs between the two. Always design in CMYK and request a printed colour proof before approving a large run. Waves Printing Press can provide proofs for colour-sensitive jobs.
What is bleed and why is it important for printing in Kuwait?
Bleed is an extra 3mm of image or colour extending beyond the final trim line of your document. Paper can shift slightly during cutting, and without bleed, this would leave a white edge on your printed piece. Always add 3mm bleed to any document that has colour, images, or design elements reaching the edge.
Can Waves Printing Press check my files before printing?
Yes. Our pre-press team reviews every file before it goes to press. If we identify issues with resolution, colour mode, bleed, or font embedding, we will contact you before printing.
